About

The Person Behind the Data

Evolving Longevity Est. 2026 Dallas, Texas

My name is Zach. I'm 33, married, dad to a toddler, and I live in the Dallas area. By day I'm a Director of Operations at a national sports retail company — I run ops across seven locations, which means my brain is wired for systems, KPIs, process optimization, and making decisions from data instead of gut feelings.

That same operating system runs my health.

Why I Do This

There are four things that will most likely kill you or strip away everything that makes life worth living: metabolic disease, heart disease, cancer, and neurodegenerative decline. Peter Attia calls them the Four Horsemen. They're responsible for the vast majority of chronic suffering and premature death in the developed world — and most of them are influenced by decisions you make every single day.

I'm not running from death. I'm running toward the version of myself that doesn't give those four horsemen an easy win.

The Moment It Started

After my son Emmett was born, something shifted. It wasn't dramatic — I just walked into the gym one day and decided I wasn't going to quit. That was it. No grand plan, no transformation montage. Just consistency.

Consistency turned into curiosity. I started watching fitness content, studying physiology, building a real training program instead of wandering around the gym. Then nutrition. Then supplementation. Then wearable tracking. Then blood panels. Each layer revealed the next question, and each question had a measurable answer. That's when I realized — this is biohacking. Not in the Silicon Valley ice-bath influencer sense. In the engineering sense. Understand the system, measure the inputs, optimize the outputs.

Today I track 41 biomarkers, wear two devices 24/7, run quarterly blood panels, and maintain a protocol designed to compound improvements over decades. My functional age is 23.5. My chronological age is 33. The gap between those numbers is the scoreboard.

The Bigger Vision

I want to be the supreme version of myself. But this isn't just about me.

I believe every human being has the capacity to be a fundamentally better version of themselves — physically, cognitively, metabolically. And I believe that when enough people pursue that, something bigger shifts. If we can get past the diseases and the decline that drain our potential, we free up the collective bandwidth of our species to focus on what actually matters: progress, discovery, solving the problems that hold us all back.

That's why I publish my data openly. That's why I built this site. Not because my protocol is the only way — but because shared, honest, measured data moves everyone forward. One person tracking their biometrics is a personal experiment. A thousand people sharing theirs is a dataset that could change how we understand human optimization.

The name isn't accidental. Evolving Longevity. We can evolve. I want to help us evolve. And every data point — mine and yours — is a step in that direction.

How Reviews Work

Step 1 — Baseline. Before a product enters my routine, I snapshot current biometrics: HRV trends, sleep architecture, recovery averages, and relevant blood markers.

Step 2 — Minimum 30-day trial. No "first impression" reviews. Most products get 60-90 days. Some devices I've tracked for over a year.

Step 3 — Data comparison. Pre vs post across WHOOP recovery, Oura readiness, sleep scores, and blood panels when applicable. If I can't measure it objectively, I note it as subjective and label it clearly.

Step 4 — Honest write-up. If a product doesn't move the needle, I say so — even when the affiliate commission is significant.
